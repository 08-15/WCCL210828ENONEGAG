Welcome to your new Mailbox experience

Let your personal assistant explain the functions


Hello my Name Is Marie; As your new assistant, let me briefly explain how we can work together smoothly and efficiently.
I am always there for you and offer you, among other things, a secure PREVIEW of messages from unknown senders. You can recognize these messages by the format of the subject line, which always begins with my name, for example:

Marie: [info@e-mail.xing.com]

The subject line always starts with my name and contains the sender's email address in brackets such as: [abc@unknown.com].

Therefore, my messages are easy to spot to reassure you that these previews are safe and that the content is presented with no active features such as clickable links, or attachments.
So you already know that it is a message from someone who is not in your active contact list. Hence, you should study them carefully (especially the sender's email address) in order to make a decision whether it is an important letter, or just spam, or even a scam. If you're not sure and the message doesn't seem important, you'd better delete it. The same sender has the option of writing you another e-mail before it is automatically blocked (by default setting).

Tip: An important indicator is the format and content of the sender's e-mail address: Does it look normal and does it relate to the content of the message?

If your check comes to a positive result, you will need the original message (which we have quarantined for your protection) and want to activate the sender so that their next e-mail is immediately delivered to your inbox.

How to activate an unknown sender

Please note: while your account was being registered, it created two sub-folders for your mailbox:

One is called "#Activate", the other "#Block-URL". These two folders are your active command channels.

To activate a new sender, simply move the associated preview message to the #Activate folder (e.g. by dragging and dropping) and wait until the original message from the sender shows-up in your inbox.

How to block a complete URL

Some spammers use their address URL with countless sender names to repeatedly send messages under different names. If you have this experience and want to get rid of this nuisance quickly, you can block the sender's URL. Just move a single preview of such a sender to #Block-URL. Already done!

Please note: Never move a preview, e.g. from a Gmail, Yahoo etc. sender, to #Block-URL, as this will block all users of this service. If you've done something wrong with a URL block, no problem. Simply delete the corresponding preview from the   #Block-URL folder.

How to block an active single sender (new feature update 12/2021)

If you have an "old" spammer, which was active before you installed your APP, that spammer was activated at the time of account registering. Thus, that spammer can send you mails still. But now it is easy to block that guy. Simply shift one of its arriving mails into the #Block/URL folder. That will set the sender on the block list. All done.



Other functions: In addition to these two direct channels, you can send me an email for all my other functionalities by writing an email to your own email address (your own account). Simply enter your command in the subject line in the required format, which is described below.

Here each command is explained in a separate step (further functions will follow).

Important: The examples below show my name "Marie" and you will need to replace this with the name of your personal assistant that will appear in your welcome message. Thanks very much.


Less often used features via mail commands

How to use MAIL commands:

1. Make a "NEW EMAIL" and address it to your own email account
2. Put the requested command in the reference line as shown below
3. Just push the SEND button. That's all


Activate a sender manually:

Marie:register(abc@missingsender.com)

After the assistant name and colon, just put a missing account-name into brackets.
To activate more than one sender, put them between the brackets, separated by comma and space like:
Marie:register(abc@missingsender.com, another@happy.de)

Change Preview counter:

Marie:3

Numbers of Previews send before blocking a sender (e.g. 3 will send 3 times a Preview before blocking)
Note: Setting 0 switch off the Auto-blocking completely, means you'll get a Preview for any unknown senders' message and no sender will be blocked! 

Command list will be continued upon finalizing...

Outlook example as a view

Your normal email communication should be as usual, but:

● If a mail from an unknown sender arrives, that message will be replaced by a safe preview, sent by your mailbox assistant and appear in your inbox. It is clearly marked with it's special subject line format, showing assistant name and senders' email address.

You can study the message without caution, as it will not have any active links or other dangerous components included.

If the message is accepted, by you, just shift the preview mail in the #Activate folder (under the Inbox folder).

Shortly after, the original mail will be shown in the Inbox.



Please note:

● Due to safety reasons, attachments, if there had been any, will not be delivered within the activation mail.


Outlook might not show the new controls

● For Microsoft Outlook you have to assign these folders in order to make them visible. 
 
1. Right-click on inbox and select IMAP folders in the menu 
2. A window pops up, please click on Query 
3. Now you should see all available folder, including Inbox.#Active and Inbox.#Block-URL 
4. Click these folders and click the Subscribe button as well, an Icon should be shown in front of each folder now 
5. Finally click Apply and OK buttons

● Note: Most email clients showing the new sub-folders, which we need for the main functions. If not, there might be special setting, similar as shown above. Please check it out with the application supplier
